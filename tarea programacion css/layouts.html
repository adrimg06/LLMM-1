Edgar Allan Poe

Best known for his poems and short fiction, Edgar Allan Poe, born in Boston, Jan. 19, 1809, is often credited as the inventor of modern detective fiction.

He was born an orphan, raised in poverty, and lived a life marked by personal tragedy, influencing his tales of mystery and macabre.

  • The Black Cat (1843)
